# **D&D 2024 Homebrew Balance Report**
**Project:** Legends of the Elsaither  
**Classes Analyzed:** Savaroen (Full Caster/Hybrid), Shadow Warrior (Nyxid)  
**Comparison Set:** D&D 2024 Standard Classes (PHB)

---

## **1. Executive Summary**

Your homebrew classes have been rebalanced to fit within the "High Power" tier of D&D 2024. They are mechanically distinct but mathematically comparable to the strongest standard options (Paladin, Cleric, Wizard).

*   **Savaroen:** **A-Tier (High).** A "Gestalt" style class that blends Full Casting with martial competency. It rivals the **Bard (College of Swords)** and **Cleric (War Domain)** in versatility.
*   **Shadow Warrior (Single Mode):** **A-Tier (High).** A durable Half-Caster with high sustain. Comparable to a **Paladin (Vengeance)** or **Ranger (Gloom Stalker)**.
*   **Shadow Warrior (Dual Mode):** **S+ Tier (Broken/Boss).** As intended, this mode overpowers any standard PC due to double Action Economy.

## **2. 1v1 Duel Simulations (Level 20)**

*Simulated matchups assuming optimal play, standard equipment, and no prep time.*

### **Savaroen vs. The Meta**
*   **Vs. Barbarian (Totem/Zealot):** **Win (60%).**
    *   *Reason:* The Savaroen's *Elemental Surge* allows for kiting, and spells like *Hold Monster* or *Fly* bypass the Barbarian's Rage defenses. The Barbarian wins if they win Initiative and grapple.
*   **Vs. Fighter (Battle Master):** **Draw (50%).**
    *   *Reason:* A pure DPS race. The Fighter's *Action Surge* deals massive burst (150+ dmg), but the Savaroen's *Shield* spell and *Mirror Image* mitigate this. It comes down to who lands the first heavy hit.
*   **Vs. Wizard (Bladesinger/War):** **Loss (40%).**
    *   *Reason:* The Wizard has a wider spell list (*Counterspell*, *Dispel Magic*, *Wall of Force*) that shuts down the Savaroen's hybrid playstyle.
*   **Vs. Paladin (Vengeance):** **Win (55%).**
    *   *Reason:* The Savaroen can match the Paladin's AC but out-ranges them with spells. *Elemental Philosophy (Balance)* neutralizes the Paladin's burst Smite damage.

### **Nyxid (Single) vs. The Meta**
*   **Vs. Rogue (Assassin):** **Win (70%).**
    *   *Reason:* *Unarmored Defense* (10+Dex+Cha) makes the Nyxid hard to hit. *Shadow Sight* prevents the Rogue from hiding.
*   **Vs. Monk (Shadow):** **Loss (45%).**
    *   *Reason:* The Monk forces too many Con saves (Stunning Strike) for the Nyxid to handle, and *Evasion* negates the Nyxid's AoE spells.
*   **Vs. Warlock (Hexblade):** **Win (60%).**
    *   *Reason:* The Nyxid has better sustain (*Blood-Siphon*) and equal burst.

## **3. Scenario Stress Tests**

### **Scenario A: The Horde (Level 5)**
*Situation: The party is surrounded by 20 Goblins.*
*   **Savaroen (Ninji):** **Excellent.** *Circular Pulse* (Fireball equivalent) clears the board instantly.
*   **Nyxid (Ravager):** **Good.** High AC (17+) means Goblins hit on 18-20. Can tank the horde indefinitely while draining HP.
*   **Comparison:** Performs better than a Fighter, equal to a Cleric/Wizard.

### **Scenario B: The Boss (Level 10)**
*Situation: A Young Red Dragon (CR 10).*
*   **Savaroen (Warrion):** **Good.** *Elemental Trance* grants Resistance to the Dragon's physical attacks. *Improved Surge* adds consistent DPS.
*   **Nyxid (Void-Arachnid):** **Poor.** The Dragon's high Strength save negates *Web* and *Restrain* abilities. The Dragon's flight forces the Nyxid to rely on weak ranged options.
*   **Comparison:** Slightly weaker than a Paladin (who has Aura of Protection), but stronger than a Monk.

### **Scenario C: The Dungeon Crawl (Resource Management)**
*Situation: 6 encounters per day, 2 Short Rests.*
*   **Savaroen:** **Excellent.** *Elemental Philosophy* and *Surge* recharge on Short Rests (via Font of Power). They never run out of gas.
*   **Nyxid:** **Moderate.** Relies heavily on Long Rest spell slots. Once out of spells, they are just a worse Fighter.

## **5. Campaign Simulation: Lost Mine of Phandelver (Lvl 1-5)**

*   **Early Game (Lvl 1-2):**
    *   The **Nyxid's** AC (16-17 starting) makes them the MVP of the "Goblin Arrows" ambush. They cannot die.
    *   The **Savaroen** is squishy until Level 2 (*Elemental Philosophy* healing comes online).
*   **Mid Game (Lvl 3-4):**
    *   **Savaroen (Warrion)** dominates the "Redbrand Hideout." *Trance* creates a nearly unkillable tank.
    *   **Nyxid (Mind-Eater)** trivializes the "Nothic" encounter by resisting its gaze and dominating it.
*   **Late Game (Lvl 5 - The Black Spider):**
    *   **Savaroen** gets *Fireball* (Circular Pulse) and *Extra Attack*. They out-damage the entire rest of the party combined.
    *   **Nyxid** gets *Extra Attack*. They become a reliable front-liner.

**Verdict:** Both classes will feel "Heroic" and slightly stronger than the pre-generated characters, but they will not break the module.

---

## **6. Final Recommendations**

1.  **Party Composition:** A party of 4 Savaroens covers every role (Warrion=Tank, Ninji=Blaster, Techno=Rogue/Utility, Ninjarikan=Striker). They are self-sufficient.
2.  **DM Warning:** The **Mind-Eater Nyxid** and **Warrion Savaroen** are the "Power Gamer" picks. Watch out for them in social encounters (Mind Control) and Boss Fights (Unkillable Tank).
3.  **Campaign Pacing:** Ensure you enforce the "6 Encounters per Day" rule. If you let the Savaroen Long Rest after every fight, their spell versatility will overshadow everyone else.
