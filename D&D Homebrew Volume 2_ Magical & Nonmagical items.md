# **Items**

Power Crystal:

Useful crafting items, as well as ammunition for element-powered weapons. When used to forge or modify a weapon, the weapon gains a \+0 magical bonus (overcoming resistance to non-magical attacks) and deals an additional **1d4 elemental damage** of the crystal's type. When used to forge or modify armor or a shield, the wearer gains **Resistance** to the crystal's elemental damage type.

These crystals are formed naturally in caves on Silveronian planets, but can also be created by spending 1 hour of focused work during a Long Rest using Fusion Smith’s Tools and consumes one non-magical gemstone worth at least 10 gp. The crystal's type matches the creator's element, or roll on an element table if non-elemental. The type of power crystal that is formed is dependent on your element type, or, if you are a regular mage, you can roll on the elements table.

The crystal of the wielder’s type can also be used in place of Crystalarium for various things.

Power crystals can also be used as ammunition in weapons of Silveronian origin. One crystal is enough to charge fifteen shots on a handheld weapon, but only one shot on a vehicle mounted or other large weapon.

Crystalarium Crystal (fully mature): 

A useful crafting item. When used to forge or modify a weapon, if the wielder belongs to an elemental lineage matching one of the weapon's damage types, they can choose for the weapon's attacks to deal half of its damage as that elemental type and half as the weapon's normal type. The weapon counts as magical. When used to forge or modify armor, if the wearer belongs to an elemental lineage, they **lose any Vulnerability** they possess to one predetermined opposing element (e.g., Fire loses Water vulnerability). They take normal damage instead of double damage from that specific element type.

Crystalarium Shard:

Useless as a crafting item. Due to its immaturity, it cannot be used to fully conduct elemental power yet.

Crystalarium Dust:

Can be mixed into food or drink. A creature consuming it must succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw or take 1 point of poison damage and suffer a minor, random elemental effect for 1 minute (roll 1d6: 1-tingling sparks (Lightning), 2-mild warmth (Fire), 3-skin feels damp (Water), 4-faint earthy smell (Earth), 5-slight chill (Cold), 6-brief nausea (Poison/Acid/Necrotic)). Elementals are immune.

Crystalarium Coin:

A currency worth 10 gp, used exclusively with Krenpowen and Tenebrie. Can be converted to crystalarium dust. Abbreviated cc.

Crystalarium:

One fully mature crystal (cy) is worth 5 shards (cs), a shard is worth 5 piles of dust (cd), and a pile of dust is equal to a coin (cc). A single seed will grow five fully mature crystals, and a fully mature crystal can be planted as a seed. The plant (ca) takes approximately one month to grow to the immature shard stage, and a full year to reach maturity. When in the shard stage, only one shard can be harvested. When mature, five crystals can be harvested.

# **Plant Life**

Tronstin Arbis \- The Tronstin Arbis is a tree known for its beautifully long and sturdy leaves. The leaves are long and green, and they are filled with a substance similar to what the trees of Warrion incorporate into their bark. This substance makes the leaves extremely sturdy and also malleable when heated, similar to forgeable metals. It is for this reason that those who specialize in earth magic like to grow these trees. Their leaves are a beautiful deep green, and they can be forged to create some of the sharpest swords and toughest armor available. The leaf blade is one of the most revered weapons in earth magic culture because of its ability to cut through almost any substance. The wood of the tree itself isn’t used for much, as the substance is only found in the leaves. The tree appears as a very wide but somewhat short trunk, as these trees only get about 12 feet tall, but their branches start towards the bottom of the tree and arc up towards the sky as they grow. This gives the tree a unique appearance, as the branches grow all the way up the tree but curve towards the sky.

Leaves can be worked like metal using Smith's Tools by someone proficient with them. Requires intense heat. Can be used as a material component to craft weapons or armor. Armor crafted primarily from these leaves grants AC 14 \+ Dex modifier (max 2\) (like Half Plate, but maybe lighter weight \- 25 lbs). Swords crafted from these leaves might function as \+1 Weapons due to their sharpness

Peibinu Arsib \- The Peibinu Arsib is a small bush known for its fire berries.This bush grows in lush forest environments and lesser deserts, but can also be found in the gardens of fire magic users. The berries that these bushes grow are shaped like a small flame, and they are considered a delicacy by those who practice fire magic. The berries have slight healing properties to anyone who eats them, and they also have a delicious taste that is a slightly imbalanced blend of spicy and sweet. Depending on the ripeness of the fruit, it may be mostly sweet or mostly spicy. If harvested early, they are oftentimes not yet filled with the chemical that causes the spice, and this is how most non-fire people eat them, however, at this stage, the fruits healing properties are negligible. It is only the spiciest of berries that have the most healing.

Eating a ripe (spicy) berry as an action restores **1d4 hit points**. Eating multiple berries within 1 hour doesn't increase the healing. Early (sweet) berries restore only 1 hit point.

Bonarka Arbis \- The Bonarka Arbis is a rather large and unassuming tree that grows on the edges of icy territories. This tree looks like any other tree from the outside, large thick trunks, dull gray-brown bark, and small green leaves that hang off of branches that grow from the top of the tree. However, the magic comes when this tree is cut, as the wood glows brightly with blue light. The wood in these trees is particularly attracted to ice magic, and is extremely useful for ice magic applications. The wood itself carries an icy appearance, and anything that touches it, aside from fire weapons, will be subjected to an effect similar to liquid nitrogen. When this wood is handled correctly, it can be used to forge blades for great axes and heads for war hammers, which also subject their enemies to large amounts of ice magic. This makes them popular for weapons wielded by ice magic warriors, as the wood is basically a curse to anyone else.

Wood glows blue. Weapons forged (Great axe/Warhammer head) deal normal weapon damage \+ **1d4 cold damage**. On a critical hit, the target must succeed on a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or have its speed **halved** until the end of its next turn. Creatures touching the raw wood must make a DC 10 Con save or take 1d6 cold damage.

Peir Dirsch \- The Peir Dirsch is a small shrub that emanates the glow of fire. It has no real practical uses, other than it is a marvel to look at and is useful for keeping away predators, as the fiery glow seems to ward them off.

Aqueirai Arbis \- The Aqueirai Arbis is a rather large tree that somewhat resembles a weeping willow tree. However, unlike the weeping willow, these trees only grow underwater, in the deepest depths of the ocean, and they also grow to be about 45 feet tall. Their bark glows with a bright blue energy, and is often used by water magic users for magic conductive armor. The wood of the tree can be used to make weapons, but when forged in thin layers, it tends to be rather… fluid-like. This makes it extremely useful for absorbing blows with thicker shields or armor, but for a sword it is rather floppy and useless for the most part.

Bark can be crafted into armor (requires appropriate tools/proficiency). Armor provides **AC 13 \+ Dex modifier** and grants **Advantage on saving throws against spells that deal Cold or Lightning damage**. Wood forged into shields grants **\+2 AC** and allows the wielder to use a **Reaction** once per short rest to **reduce incoming bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage by 1d10**.

Warrionic Arbis Stinshaka \- The Arbis Stinshaka is a massive tree that grows in the forests of Warrion. These trees are very strong and have a slightly metallic look to them, not in the traditional sense, but in that the bark is stronger than most metals. This wood, when it can be cut, is used for a massive variety of things, as these trees grow all over the forests of Warrion. Spaceships, armor, shields, the handles of weaponry, other vehicles, bowls, utensils, houses, and a number of other things are all crafted from the wood of these trees. The wood is very lightweight, but is extremely durable, to the point where it can withstand the negative pressure of space and the pressure of the deepest depths of the ocean without changing form. Powerful earth abilities are required to manipulate these trees however, because of their almost indestructible nature.

Objects made from it have **double standard HP and Resistance to all damage**. Requires magical means or very high Strength checks (DC 25+) to break/cut. Manipulation requires specific high-level magic or plot device tools

Powenantan Arbis \- The Powenanten Arbis is a small tree that grows randomly in the middle of lush forests. These trees usually grow a mix of crystalarium and elemental gems, and are quite a rarity to actually find. Their beauty is unmatched by any other plant in Silveronia. These trees have a powerful elemental glow that goes all the way up its bark and into the crystals that grow from its branches. It is quite a sight to see. These trees could probably open up many possibilities for new technologies and research, if they weren’t completely indestructible. No one has yet figured out how to cut these trees down, as they seem to be shielded by a combination of all of the elements. The crystals however, can be picked, and have the properties of regular elemental gems and crystalarium, as well as strong healing abilities.

The crystals function as standard Power Crystals/Crystalarium Crystals. Additionally, consuming a crystal restores hit points as if drinking a **Potion of Greater Healing (4d4+4 HP)**. Crystals respawn at 1d4 per month

Peirusen Arbis \- The Peirusen Arbis is a large, thick tree that grows in volcanic areas, and seems to always be on fire. These trees are highly sought after for their wood, which is magically conductive towards fire magic. The wood also has an interesting property of being slightly magnetic, and if the right amount of heat and metal are forged into the wood, it will be able to magnetically lock with other pieces of wood that are similarly treated. This same strange magnetic connection can also be manipulated by fire magic users. This makes the wood very useful for creating fire shields, as the shield can be expanded and shrunk down at will, creating a very versatile weapon. The wood of this tree is also very heavy, which is why it is not used particularly often. The trees themselves are also a rather rare find.

Wood is magically conductive for fire spells (e.g., allows rerolling 1s on fire damage dice for spells cast through a focus made of this wood). Magnetic property is specific to the Peirushard item (see below). Wood is **Heavy** (items crafted weigh 1.5x normal).

Aqueio Hundirsch \- The Aqueio Hundirsch is a small shrub that grow long tendrils that reach up towards the sky, though they are only about 3 feet tall. At the ends of these tendrils grows a small, onion-like bulb, which, when cut open, has a dragon fruit like flesh that appears blue in color. If left to seed, these tendrils will fall and plant themselves, making this wandering onion clone an invasive, but very useful species. The fruits from this strange plant are infused with elemental water energy, making them very healing and highly nutritious, but slightly psychedelic. Water magic users have reported hallucinating for several days, sleeping for three straight days afterward, and then feeling better than they have ever felt in their entire lives prior to the slightly strange and painful experience. Because of their psychedelic nature, these fruits are not eaten often, but because of their intense healing properties, they are used by doctors and druids that practice water magic.

Eating a bulb restores **4d4+4 HP** and provides sustenance for 24 hours. The consumer must succeed on a **DC 15 Wisdom saving throw** or suffer effects similar to the **Confusion** spell for **1d4 hours**, followed by extreme lethargy (speed halved, disadvantage on Str/Dex checks/saves) for 8 hours.

Gruhunsh \- The Gruhunsh is a strange vegetable that grows much like a potato, but it has the appearance of a coconut that is still covered in its skin. This strange food has a slight glow, and is filled with earth magic. This makes it very hard on the outside, which requires a hammer to remove. Once the shell is broken and removed, the inside reveals a soft, brown, melon-like flesh, which tastes similar to coffee beans. This strange fruit is highly energizing and has a caffeine like effect, if said caffeine was on steroids. This can cause heart problems and/or death in any magic user who does not use earth or lightning magic.

Consuming the flesh grants the benefits of the **Haste** spell (no concentration required) for **1 minute**. Non-Earth/Lightning elementals (or specify creature types/classes) must make a **DC 15 Constitution saving throw** immediately after consumption and again after 1 minute. On a failed save, they suffer **3 levels of Exhaustion**.

Sladensh Arbis \- The Sladensh Arbis is a large tree that looks much like an apple tree, except it doesn’t grow apples. The fruit grown by this tree is round, covered in dragon-like scales, and emits an orange-red glow, similar to laser magic. Occasionally, these fruits also glow with a golden hue, which is slightly more sweet in taste than the red ones, which are also mildly sweet in taste. These fruits are lightly energizing and increase mental focus, however, they increase the mental focus to an extreme degree. This will increase the number of brain waves by more than double of what they were prior to eating the fruit, as the fruit appears to cause temporary intelligence boosts. Studies have shown that people that had eaten the fruit less than one hour before performing a task that requires mental skill, such as a memory game, chess, video games, and intense debates, had a 300% performance increase versus when they ate the fruit an more than one hour before or if they had not eaten the fruit at all. Eating more than one of these fruits does not increase the effect, and it appears that the effect can only happen once every 24 hours.

Eating a fruit grants **Advantage on Intelligence checks and saving throws** for **1 hour**. This effect occurs only once per 24 hours.

Shokunsch \-  The Shokunsch is a melon fruit that has a rough texture and emits a slight static aura. When touched, you will be slightly shocked, which is not harmful, but can be a scare to someone who is not used to it. These melons are extremely hard on the outside, and the faster they move, the more electrical charge they will build. They also taste like watermelon, but with a slight iron-y taste. These fruits are not commonly eaten, but instead are used as cannon projectiles, because when fired from a cannon, the electrical charge gained by the fruit is enough to form chain lightning when the fruit impacts its target. The fruit is dark yellow in appearance and grows to be similar in size to a standard cannonball, or a small watermelon.

As an improvised thrown weapon (range 20/60), deals 1d4 bludgeoning \+ 1d6 lightning damage. If used as ammunition for a cannon or catapult, on impact it explodes: Creatures within 10 feet must make a **DC 13 Dexterity saving throw**, taking **3d6 lightning damage** on a fail, half on success. The original target has disadvantage on the save.

Oxyrabinu \- Oxyrabinun are small berries that grow in a similar fashion to grapes and blueberries. Clustered in bunches, but grows on a bush. These strange little fruits are roughly the size of a large grape, and are pure white in appearance. They have zero taste whatsoever, but are quite nutritious and filling. The only downside of this fruit is that it is extremely psychedelic, however this effect only lasts for 15 minutes. The fruit is also filled with enough nutrients to power on the body for three days without other food or drink, but the psychedelic effect is similar to taking every drug available on the market, doubling it, and somehow not dying. This has no lasting effect on the brain or body, other than the trauma of being essentially drugged with enough power to overdose roughly 8,000 elephants.

Eating one berry provides enough nourishment for 3 days. The consumer must succeed on a **DC 15 Wisdom saving throw** or be **Incapacitated** for 1 minute as they experience intense hallucinations, followed by **Disadvantage on all ability checks, saving throws, and attack rolls** for 1 hour.

Darkia Hunsh \- Darkia Hunsh are little fruits that are similar in texture to kiwi fruits, and have large black leaves around the base that are similar to the leaves of a pineapple in shape only. The leaves are very soft, but have a sting when you touch them. The fruit itself grows similarly to a watermelon or pumpkin. The fruit, once full grown, is a very deep, dark purple, with a bluish glow towards the base, where the leaves attach to the melon. When eaten, these fruits will create very dark thoughts in the consumer’s mind, opening up their deepest, darkest desires and working similar to a truth serum. If taken in high doses, the effects of this plant are slightly changed in that they will drive the consumer mad through evil thoughts revolving around murder, theft, destruction, and other various crimes and atrocities. If taken in lower doses, the truth serum effect is mild and as such, this plant is often grown by Tenebrie for just that: a truth serum.

Low Dose (one fruit): Consumer has **Disadvantage on Charisma (Deception) checks** and must succeed on a **DC 12 Wisdom saving throw** to tell a deliberate lie for 1 hour. High Dose (2+ fruits): Consumer must make a **DC 14 Wisdom saving throw** or suffer from **Short-Term Madness** (DMG p. 259\) for 1d10 minutes, with effects flavored towards paranoia and harmful impulses.

Daelosbinu \- Daelosbinu are small berries that are deceptively similar to blackberries. They look like blackberries, they grow on a bush that looks almost exactly the same as blackberries. When disturbed, these berries have a slight purple-red glow. When eaten, these berries have a bloody taste with a hint of lemon, and are EXTREMELY deadly to anyone who has an element other than death. The effect that these berries have when consumed is a slow and painful one. If you don’t die from puking your guts out, or from the intense hallucinations and the passing out every six or seven minutes, then you will die when your organs start exploding after about half an hour of agonizing pain. Yes. Your organs will explode. Not massively or violently, but they will start bursting like a bad appendix and the internal bleeding will quickly snuff your life out. The only way to survive eating these little demon fruits is to not eat them. Period.

Consumer must make a **DC 16 Constitution saving throw**. On failure, they take **6d6 poison damage** and are **Poisoned** for 24 hours. While poisoned, they also suffer intense hallucinations (Disadvantage on Wis checks/saves) and must repeat the save every hour, taking another 3d6 poison damage on a failure. If the save fails 3 times, they die. Success ends the hourly damage but not the Poisoned condition.

Cutlen Hunsh \- Cutlen Hunsh are large fruits that have a similar look to a pineapple without the leaves. The sections of the pineapple part are a bit more spiky than a standard pineapple, and the fruits themselves are usually a swirl of silver and black. These fruits grow on short trees and are exceptionally easy to eat, as it is as if the fruit has already been pre-sliced, while still holding together on the tree. The fruits have a sweet, fruity, blood taste, and have a reputation for causing severe gum bleeds. They don’t deteriorate the gums or cause any lasting damage, but you will bleed for a really long time. This can be unnerving, may cause nausea, and probably contributes to the strange taste of the fruit. No one is entirely sure why it does this, and some speculate that it is the fruit itself that is bleeding. That being said, they are quite delicious, aside from the blood taste, and are a favorite in Tenebrie cuisine. Just don’t eat too many. They are hallucinogenic if you eat more than two fruits.

Taste is unusual. Causes harmless but noticeable gum bleeding for 10 minutes (no HP loss, possibly Disadvantage on Cha checks involving speech?). Eating more than two fruits requires a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** to avoid effects similar to the **Confusion** spell for 1 minute.

Duchina Restraints

Adventuring Gear, Common

Weight: 4lbs  
Cost: 2 cc

Effect: These restraints are designed in a way to restrict power usage and movement quickly and efficiently. They can be opened or closed fairly easily, but are very strong, due to the nature of duchina. These restraints are locked and unlocked using fusion matrices, and are locked to the power of whatever crystal is found inside the fusion matrix. This makes for a very easy escape when using a crystalarium filled matrix, because the restraint is not locked to any particular power. It also makes it much easier to lock and unlock for whoever is using the device, however. These restraints work to suppress powers by blocking the waves from the user, once again due to the nature of duchina, however because it is such a small device only covering the hands, the suppression can only be pushed so far. Escaping the restraints requires a successful DC 15 Dex (Sleight of Hand) check as an action. Bursting them requires a successful DC 17 Str (Athletics) check as an action. Failed escape attempts will cause 1d4 bludgeoning damage to the wearer.

* *Contracted:* Standard Shield (+2 AC), normal speed.

  * *Expanded:* Bonus action to expand. Grants **\+3 AC**, speed is halved.

  * *Wall:* Action to expand further. Grants **Three-Quarters Cover (+5 AC)** to user and creatures directly behind it (5ft wide). User's speed becomes 0\. Reverts to Contracted if user moves.

  * *Sword:* Bonus action to transform. Becomes a **Longsword \+1** that deals an additional **1d6 fire damage**. No AC bonus in this form.

Plot Device

It can survive in any environment, due to its crystalarium carbide hull, and takes 1 action to arm. # **Augments**

Ninjonian armor can have up to three augments equipped, with the exception of Royal Class armor, which can have four. These augments are attachments and upgrades that customize and improve armor in varying ways, depending on the augment.

Assassin’s Cloak Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Assassin’s Cloak is a small armor augment that increases stealth by \+2, and can be installed on any Ninjonian armor.

Ninja’s Cloak Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Ninja’s Cloak is a small armor augment that increases stealth by \+1, and can be installed on any Ninjonian armor.

Warrior’s Well Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Warrior’s Well is a small armor augment that allows you to use an Action to recover one expended 1st-level spell slot. Once used, this property can't be used again until the next dawn. It can be installed on any Ninjonian armor.

Juggernaut’s Resilience Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Juggernaut’s Resilience is a small armor augment that increases your max HP by an amount equal to your level while the armor is worn, and can be installed on any Ninjonian Armor.

Engineer’s Belt Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Engineer’s Belt is a small armor augment that passively constructs 1 Silveronian (chosen race) grenade per day (at half material cost), up to a max capacity of two, and can be installed on any Ninjonian Armor.

Royal’s Well Armor Augment  
Tier 1 Armor Augment

The Royal’s Well is a small armor augment that allows you to use an Action to recover up to two expended 1st-level spell slots (or one 2nd-level slot). Once used, this property can't be used again until the next dawn. It can be installed on any Ninjonian armor.
